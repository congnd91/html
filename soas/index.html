Corporate Services

Corporate Formation

Incorporation is the legal process used to form a corporate entity or company. A corporation is the resulting legal entity that separates the ﬁrm's assets and income from its owners and investors.

Directorship Services

Directors' responsibilities. Company directors are responsible for the management of their companies. They must act honestly and promote the success of the business and beneﬁt its shareholders. Directorship services serves to meet client’s need to meet regulatory requirement to maintain a resident director in their companies.

Shareholder Trust Services

Shareholder trust service is an arrangement that allows a third party or trustee hold corporate shares for a beneﬁciary or beneﬁciaries to meet local ownership requirements.

Corporate Secretarial Services

Within any organization, a Corporate Secretary's duties include ensuring the integrity of the governance framework, being responsible for the efﬁcient administration of a company, ensuring compliance with statutory and regulatory requirements and implementing decisions made by the Board of Directors.

Registered Ofﬁce Services

A registered ofﬁce is the ofﬁcial address of an incorporated company, association or any other legal entity. Generally it will form part of the public record and is required in most countries where the registered organization or legal entity is incorporated.

Accounting & Compliance

Payroll Services

Payroll services involves handling all payroll functions to reduce the need for in house trained payroll staff and maintenance of appropriate payroll software and staying compliant with labour regulations.

Business Process

Business process outsourcing (BPO) is the contracting of a speciﬁc business task, such as payroll, to a third-party service provider. Usually, BPO is implemented as a cost-saving measure for tasks that a company requires but does not depend upon to maintain their position in the marketplace.

Payment and Collection Services

Proper controls and payment authorization over expenditure bills  and close monitoring of collections of revenue receivables is essential to ensure cash ﬂows are well managed..

Financial Reporting

Financial reporting is the process of summarising and presenting accounting output as a report such as an income statement or a balance sheet to provide company’s shareholders or management view of ﬁnancial performance and position of the company for purpose of making corporate decisions.

Accounting

Accounting services is the process of collecting, tracking and recording of all business transactions in a systematic method congruent with accounting standards and practices adopted by the country where company is domiciled.

Financial Statement Compilation

In a compilation engagement, the chartered accountant summarizes accounting data provided by the client and presents them as ﬁnancial statements supported with notes and disclosures according to ﬁnancial reporting standards adopted by the company and issues these as report issued by the board of directors to shareholders.

Internal Audit

Internal Controls

Internal control, as deﬁned by accounting and auditing, is a process for assuring of an organization’s objectives in operational effectiveness and efﬁciency, reliable ﬁnancial reporting, and compliance with laws, regulations and policies.

Attestation Services

Attest is an engagement that requires independence. This includes audits, reviews, compilations, attestation and assurance services. Attestation is an engagement where a written report is issued on something other than historical ﬁnancial statements.

Data and Audit and Analytics

Use data analytics to help businesses uncover valuable insights within their ﬁnancials, identify process improvements that can increase efﬁciency, and better manage risk.

Agreed Upon Procedures

An agreed-upon procedure is a standard a company or client outlines when it hires an external party to perform an audit on a speciﬁc test or business process. The procedures, which are called audit standards, are designed and agreed upon by the entity conducting the audit, as well as any appropriate third parties.

Business Strategy & Market Growth

A market expansion growth strategy, often called market development, entails selling current products in a new market. There several reasons why a company may consider a market expansion strategy. If a business does not ﬁnd new markets for its products, it cannot increase sales or proﬁts.

Contracts & Agreements

Contract Review

Contract review is a thinking process-a rational analysis. This process includes: clarifying of contract related facts, measure of the feasibility of contract, and forecast of contract risks. Contract review and control of legal risk show the relationships between means and purpose, as well as form and substance.

Contract Drafting

Engage a Contract specialist to help draft important company contracts. A contract is legally binding agreement. Drafting a contract is the act of writing out the terms and details of the contract, in order to outline the legal obligations of the two parties, so that they fully understand the terms of the agreement and their respective duties towards one another. 

Mergers & Acquisitions

Valuations

Valuation is the analytical process of determining the current (or projected) worth of an asset or a company for use in commercial negotiations, sale or purchase of businesses or assets, and accounting purposes.

Deal Negotiations, Funding, Capitalization

Business negotiations to bring negotiating parties to closer to conclude a contractual deal ensuring both parties are satisﬁed with what is being negotiated.  Funding and capitalization refers to the capital required and sourcing of loans or funding instruments required for the deal.

Tax Due Diligence

Tax due diligence is a comprehensive examination of the different types of taxes that may be imposed upon a particular business, as well as the various taxing jurisdictions in which it may have sufﬁcient connection to be subject to such taxes. 

Structuring Acquisition & Disposition

An M&A deal structure is a binding agreement between parties in a merger or acquisition (M&A) that outlines the rights and obligations of both parties.  It states the terms and conditions of a M&A agreement and what each party of the deal is entitled to and what each is obliged to do under the agreement.

Corporate Restructuring

Corporate restructuring is a corporate action taken to signiﬁcantly modify the structure or the operations of the company. This usually happens when a company is facing signiﬁcant problems and is in ﬁnancial jeopardy.
